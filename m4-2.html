Noise: Sources, Temperature
& the Receiver Chain

MODULE 4
RF & LINK BUDGETS

The link equation from m4-1 contains G/T — a single term that summarises the entire receive chain. The denominator T_sys is not a measurement you take with a thermometer. It is a mathematical construct capturing the combined noise contribution of every component from the antenna aperture to the demodulator input: the sky brightness the antenna collects, the feed network, the LNA, the downconverter. Getting it right is the difference between a link budget that matches measured performance and one that is optimistic by 2–5 dB.

1. Thermal Noise: The Physical Origin

All matter above absolute zero emits electromagnetic radiation due to the random thermal motion of charge carriers. This Johnson-Nyquist noise sets a fundamental floor on any receiving system's sensitivity. A resistor at temperature T in bandwidth B generates:

N = k · T · B

where k = 1.380649 × 10⁻²³ J/K. This is exact and universal — it is a thermodynamic statement, not an electronics one. The noise power spectral density per unit bandwidth:

N₀ = k · T → at 290 K: N₀ ≈ −204 dBW/Hz

Every passive component in the receive chain that has loss also has an associated noise temperature. There is no way around this: loss and noise are the same physical phenomenon in a passive element.

2. Noise Figure and Noise Temperature

An amplifier adds its own noise to the signal it processes. Noise figure (NF) characterises this addition relative to a reference input at T₀ = 290 K:

T_e = T₀ · (NF_linear − 1) = 290 · (10^(NF_dB/10) − 1)
NF_dB = 10 · log₁₀(1 + T_e / T₀)

T₀ = 290 K IS A CONVENTION, NOT A TEMPERATURE The IEEE reference temperature T₀ = 290 K is an agreed standard for defining noise figure, not necessarily the physical temperature of the component. A component at 250 K physical temperature still uses T₀ = 290 K to compute NF by convention — the physical temperature enters separately via the passive-loss noise temperature formula.

3. Passive Components: Loss Equals Noise

For any passive component with insertion loss L (linear) at physical temperature T_phys:

T_e = T_phys · (L − 1) where L = 10^(loss_dB/10)

A feed network with 0.5 dB loss at 290 K: L = 1.122, T_e = 290 × 0.122 = 35 K — comparable to an excellent LNA. A 1 dB loss at 290 K adds approximately 75 K. The rule of thumb is roughly 72 K per dB of loss (for small losses at 290 K).

This is one of the most important results in receive chain design: every decibel of loss before the LNA directly adds noise at the physical temperature of that lossy element. Every passive component between the antenna aperture and the first amplifier is a noise source, not just an attenuator.

THE COST OF PRE-LNA LOSS 0.5 dB of feed loss at 290 K adds 35 K to T_sys. For a ground terminal targeting T_sys = 100 K, this single half-decibel loss is already 35% of the entire noise budget. This is why minimising the electrical path between the antenna feed point and the LNA input — ideally mounting the LNA directly at the feed — is the single highest-leverage design action in receive chain optimisation.

4. The Friis Noise Figure Formula

The total system noise temperature for a cascaded receive chain is:

T_sys = T_ant + T₁ + T₂/G₁ + T₃/(G₁·G₂) + ···

where T_ant is the antenna noise temperature, T₁ is the first stage (feed) noise temperature, T₂ is the second stage (LNA), G₁ is the first stage gain, and so on. Each subsequent stage's noise contribution is divided by the cumulative gain of all preceding stages.

Worked example

Ground terminal receive chain:

  • T_ant = 50 K (clear sky, 30° elevation)
  • Feed: 0.3 dB loss → T_feed = 20.4 K, L_feed = 1.071
  • LNA: NF = 1.0 dB → T_LNA = 74.6 K; G_LNA = 40 dB (× 10,000)
  • Downconverter: NF = 8 dB → T_2 = 1,537 K
T_sys = 50/1.071 + 20.4 + 74.6 + 1537/10000 = 46.7 + 20.4 + 74.6 + 0.15 ≈ 142 K

The downconverter's 1,537 K noise temperature contributes only 0.15 K because the LNA gain divides it by 10,000. The LNA (74.6 K) is the dominant contributor — which is exactly the design intent of the Friis architecture.

THE LNA DOMINATES — BY DESIGN The Friis formula is not just an equation; it is a design philosophy. Place the best (lowest noise figure) amplifier first. Give it as much gain as practical. Everything after it contributes negligibly to T_sys. The entire receive chain optimisation problem reduces to: minimise pre-LNA loss, minimise LNA noise figure, maximise LNA gain.

5. The Antenna Noise Temperature

T_ant is not the physical temperature of the antenna structure. It is the brightness temperature of the electromagnetic environment the antenna's radiation pattern is looking at — the weighted average of the sky brightness temperature over the entire antenna pattern.

For a ground station at Ka-band:

  • Clear sky, high elevation: dominated by the cosmic microwave background (~2.7 K) plus atmospheric oxygen and water vapour emission — typically 5–40 K above 20° elevation
  • Near horizon (10° elevation): longer atmospheric path through more air — 50–100 K
  • Ground spillover: sidelobes that illuminate the warm ground (~290 K) add 2–50 K depending on antenna sidelobe quality
  • During rain: rain radiates as a near-blackbody at ~273 K, dramatically increasing T_ant (see §6)

6. The Noise–Rain Coupling Effect

During a rain event, two things happen simultaneously, both reducing C/N₀:

  • Signal attenuation: rain absorbs the signal, reducing C by A_rain_dB
  • Noise increase: rain is a warm absorber (~273 K) that emits thermal noise, raising T_ant and reducing G/T

The increase in antenna noise temperature from rain attenuation A (linear) at rain temperature T_rain:

ΔT_ant ≈ T_rain · (1 − 1/A) ≈ 273 · (1 − 10^(−A_dB/10))

For A_dB = 10 dB: ΔT_ant ≈ 273 × 0.9 = 245 K. If the baseline T_sys was 150 K, rain raises it to 395 K — a factor of 2.6× increase in noise power, adding approximately 4.1 dB of noise degradation on top of the 10 dB signal fade. Total C/N₀ degradation: ~14.1 dB, not just 10 dB.

For systems with low baseline T_sys (sensitive ground receivers), the noise-coupling term is proportionally more damaging. For satellite receive chains where T_sys is already ~400 K (dominated by the warm Earth), the incremental rain noise is proportionally smaller.

RAIN MARGIN IS NOT JUST THE FADE DEPTH Link budgets that account only for rain signal attenuation and ignore the noise coupling underestimate the required rain margin. For a sensitive ground terminal with T_sys = 100 K, a 10 dB rain event causes approximately 14–15 dB total C/N₀ degradation — 4–5 dB more than the fade depth alone. The rain margin must cover both effects.

7. The Satellite Receive Chain

The satellite receive antenna (return link, user terminal uplink) looks at the Earth — a ~255 K blackbody filling most of its field of view. T_ant ≈ 200–290 K, much higher than a ground station looking at cold sky. This is the fundamental thermal asymmetry between the forward and return links: the satellite is always a noisier receiver than a ground terminal aimed at cold sky.

A typical LEO satellite receive chain at Ka-band:

  • T_ant = 255 K (nadir, warm Earth)
  • Feed: 0.2 dB loss → T_feed = 13.6 K
  • LNA: NF = 1.5 dB → T_LNA = 120 K; G = 35 dB
  • Downconverter: NF = 8 dB → T_2 = 1,537 K → contribution = 1537/3162 ≈ 0.49 K
T_sys ≈ 255/1.047 + 13.6 + 120 + 0.49 ≈ 378 K

Compared to the ground terminal at ~142 K, the satellite is approximately 4.3 dB noisier as a receiver. This must be compensated by higher terminal EIRP on the return link, tighter modulation thresholds, or accepted as a capacity asymmetry (forward link delivers more capacity than the return link for the same geometry).

Exercise 2 — Friis Noise Chain

Compute T_sys for ground terminal and satellite receive chains, showing each stage's contribution.

import math
T0 = 290.0

def nf_to_te(nf_db): return T0 * (10**(nf_db/10) - 1)
def loss_te(loss_db, t=T0): return t * (10**(loss_db/10) - 1)
def db_to_lin(x): return 10**(x/10)

def friis(t_ant, stages):
    """
    stages: list of (label, te_k, gain_db or None)
    Returns T_sys and per-stage contributions.
    """
    t = t_ant; cum_g = 1.0
    bd = [("Antenna", t_ant, t_ant)]
    for lbl, te, g_db in stages:
        contrib = te / cum_g
        t += contrib
        bd.append((lbl, te, contrib))
        if g_db is not None:
            cum_g *= db_to_lin(g_db)
    return t, bd

def print_chain(name, t_ant, stages):
    t, bd = friis(t_ant, stages)
    print(f"\n{name}")
    print(f"  {'Component':<30}{'Own T_e':>8}  {'Contribution':>12}")
    print("  " + "─"*52)
    for lbl, te, contrib in bd:
        bar = '▪' * min(int(contrib/4), 18)
        print(f"  {lbl:<30}{te:>7.1f}K  {contrib:>10.1f}K  {bar}")
    print("  " + "─"*52)
    print(f"  {'T_sys':>30}{'':>9}  {t:>10.1f} K")
    print(f"  NF equivalent: {10*math.log10(1+t/T0):.2f} dB")
    return t

# Ground terminal — Ka forward link
print_chain("Ground terminal (30° elevation, clear sky)",
    t_ant=30.0,
    stages=[
        ("Feed (0.3dB loss)",       loss_te(0.3),   -0.3),
        ("LNA (NF=1.0dB, G=40dB)", nf_to_te(1.0),  40.0),
        ("Downconverter (NF=8dB)",  nf_to_te(8.0),  20.0),
        ("IF chain (NF=10dB)",      nf_to_te(10.0), None),
    ])

# Satellite receive — Ka return link
print_chain("Satellite receive (nadir, warm Earth)",
    t_ant=255.0,
    stages=[
        ("Feed (0.2dB)",            loss_te(0.2),   -0.2),
        ("LNA (NF=1.5dB, G=35dB)", nf_to_te(1.5),  35.0),
        ("Downconverter (NF=8dB)",  nf_to_te(8.0),  20.0),
        ("IF chain (NF=10dB)",      nf_to_te(10.0), None),
    ])

Exercise 3 — Spine Stage 2: Friis Chain in the Link Budget Engine

Extend the LinkBudget class from Stage 1 with a computed Friis noise chain replacing the scalar T_sys. Also models noise-rain coupling.

import math
K_DB = -228.6; T0 = 290.0

def fspl(d, f): return 92.4 + 20*math.log10(d) + 20*math.log10(f)
def nf_te(nf):  return T0 * (10**(nf/10) - 1)
def loss_te(l, t=T0): return t * (10**(l/10) - 1)
def dbl(x): return 10**(x/10)

class LinkBudget:
    def __init__(self, name):
        self.name=name; self.losses=[]; self._chain=[]
        self._eirp=self._fspl=self._grx=self._tant=self._req=None

    def set_transmitter(self, p, g): self._eirp = 10*math.log10(p) + g
    def set_path(self, d, f):        self._fspl = fspl(d, f)
    def add_loss(self, lbl, v):      self.losses.append((lbl, v))
    def set_antenna(self, g, tant):  self._grx=g; self._tant=tant
    def add_stage(self, lbl, te, g=None): self._chain.append((lbl,te,g))
    def set_required(self, r):       self._req=r

    def _tsys(self):
        t=self._tant; cg=1.0
        for _,te,g in self._chain:
            t+=te/cg
            if g: cg*=dbl(g)
        return t

    def compute(self, rain_db=0.0):
        t=self._tant
        if rain_db > 0:
            t += 273 * (1 - 10**(-rain_db/10))  # noise-rain coupling
        # recompute chain with updated T_ant
        t_sys = t
        cg=1.0
        for _,te,g in self._chain:
            t_sys+=te/cg
            if g: cg*=dbl(g)
        total_loss = self._fspl + sum(v for _,v in self.losses) + rain_db
        cn0 = self._eirp - total_loss + self._grx - 10*math.log10(t_sys) - K_DB
        return cn0, cn0-self._req if self._req else None, t_sys

    def print_budget(self, rain_db=0.0, label=""):
        cn0, margin, t_sys = self.compute(rain_db)
        gt = self._grx - 10*math.log10(t_sys)
        tag = f"  [{label}]" if label else ""
        print(f"\n  {self.name}{tag}")
        print(f"  EIRP {self._eirp:+.1f}dBW  FSPL {self._fspl:.1f}dB  "
              f"T_sys {t_sys:.0f}K  G/T {gt:.1f}dB/K")
        if rain_db: print(f"  Rain: {rain_db}dB fade + noise coupling")
        print(f"  C/N₀ {cn0:.1f}dBHz  Req {self._req:.1f}  "
              f"Margin {margin:+.1f}dB  {'✓' if margin>=0 else '✗'}")

lb = LinkBudget("LEO Ka forward — Friis chain + rain coupling")
lb.set_transmitter(20, 34); lb.set_path(1000, 20.0)
lb.add_loss("Gaseous absorption", 0.3); lb.add_loss("Pointing", 0.5)
lb.set_antenna(37.5, 30.0)
lb.add_stage("Feed 0.3dB",       loss_te(0.3), -0.3)
lb.add_stage("LNA NF=1.0 G=40",  nf_te(1.0),   40.0)
lb.add_stage("Downconv NF=8",    nf_te(8.0),    20.0)
lb.add_stage("IF NF=10",         nf_te(10.0),   None)
lb.set_required(105.0)

lb.print_budget(rain_db=0,  label="clear sky")
lb.print_budget(rain_db=3,  label="3 dB rain")
lb.print_budget(rain_db=10, label="10 dB rain")
lb.print_budget(rain_db=20, label="20 dB heavy rain")
