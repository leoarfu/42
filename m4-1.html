The Link Budget:
EIRP, Path Loss & G/T

MODULE 4
RF & LINK BUDGETS

Every satellite communications system reduces to a single question: does the received signal have enough power, relative to the noise floor, to support the required data rate? The link budget is the structured accounting that answers this question — taking every gain and every loss in the signal's journey from transmitter to receiver, summing them in decibels, and comparing the result to the minimum threshold the demodulator requires. This lesson derives the link equation from first principles and builds the scaffold that the remaining six lessons will fill with atmospheric, interference, and environmental effects.

1. Decibel Arithmetic

The link budget is computed in decibels because signal power spans many orders of magnitude — a satellite transmitting 10 W at 550 km produces a received power at the terminal of approximately 10⁻¹³ W. Working in logarithms turns that fourteen-order-of-magnitude range into a manageable addition and subtraction problem.

X_dB = 10 · log₁₀(X_linear)    X_linear = 10^(X_dB / 10)

Antenna gain is expressed in dBi — decibels relative to an isotropic radiator. Power is expressed in dBW (relative to 1 W) or dBm (relative to 1 mW). 1 W = 0 dBW = 30 dBm.

2. Free-Space Path Loss

A transmitter radiates into a spherical wavefront. At distance d, power is spread over area 4πd². A receive antenna with effective area A_eff = G_R · λ²/(4π) intercepts a fraction of this. The ratio of received to transmitted power for isotropic antennas in free space — the free-space path loss — is:

FSPL = (4π d / λ)² → FSPL_dB = 92.4 + 20·log₁₀(d_km) + 20·log₁₀(f_GHz)

At Ka-band downlink (20 GHz) and 1,000 km slant range:

FSPL = 92.4 + 60.0 + 26.0 = 178.4 dB

This is the dominant loss in any satellite link — roughly 178 dB that must be overcome by EIRP and receive gain. Note the dependencies: FSPL increases as d² (6 dB per doubling of range) and as f² (+6 dB per doubling of frequency). It does not depend on transmit power or antenna design — those determine what remains after this loss is subtracted.

HIGHER FREQUENCY = MORE PATH LOSS Moving from Ku-band (14 GHz) to Ka-band (20 GHz) adds 20·log₁₀(20/14) ≈ +3.1 dB of path loss at the same range. Moving from Ka to V-band (47 GHz) adds another 20·log₁₀(47/20) ≈ +7.4 dB. Higher frequencies need more antenna gain or transmit power just to maintain the same received signal level, even before atmospheric effects are added.

3. Effective Isotropic Radiated Power (EIRP)

EIRP is the product of transmit power and transmit antenna gain:

EIRP_dBW = P_T_dBW + G_T_dBi

EIRP represents the power that an isotropic radiator would need to produce the same power flux density at the receiver. It is the single figure of merit for the transmit side of any link.

A Starlink satellite with ~20 W per beam and ~34 dBi spot beam gain: EIRP ≈ 13 + 34 = 47 dBW. A user terminal with 2 W and 35 dBi: EIRP ≈ 3 + 35 = 38 dBW. EIRP is controlled by regulatory limits (EPFD, power flux density masks) that constrain the product of power and gain — a high-gain low-power design can have the same EIRP as a low-gain high-power design.

4. The Received Carrier Power

Combining EIRP with path loss and receive gain:

C_dBW = EIRP_dBW − FSPL_dB + G_R_dBi − L_other_dB

where L_other is the sum of all additional losses: pointing, atmospheric, feed/line, rain fade. All are positive numbers that reduce C. This is the first line of the link budget — the received signal power.

5. The Noise Floor: kTB

Thermal noise power in bandwidth B at temperature T is N = k · T · B, where k = 1.380649 × 10⁻²³ J/K. The noise power spectral density (per Hz):

N₀_dBW/Hz = −228.6 + T_sys_dBK

where −228.6 = 10·log₁₀(1.380649 × 10⁻²³) and T_sys is the system noise temperature in Kelvin (covered in detail in m4-2). For a typical terminal at T_sys = 300 K (24.8 dBK):

N₀ = −228.6 + 24.8 = −203.8 dBW/Hz

6. C/N₀ — The Master Link Quality Metric

The carrier-to-noise density ratio C/N₀ = C/N₀ (linear, in Hz) is the ratio of received carrier power to noise power spectral density:

C/N₀_dBHz = C_dBW − N₀_dBW/Hz

C/N₀ is bandwidth-independent — it describes link quality in a way that allows comparison across systems with different channel widths. The required C/N₀ for a given data rate and modulation scheme is a property of the air interface (Module 5); the achieved C/N₀ is what the link budget computes.

The receive system figure of merit G/T (receive gain divided by system noise temperature) appears naturally:

C/N₀_dBHz = EIRP − FSPL − L_other + G/T + 228.6

where G/T_dB = G_R_dBi − T_sys_dBK. Higher G/T means the receiver collects more signal relative to its noise floor.

7. The Full Link Equation

The complete link equation and its derived quantities:

C/N₀ [dBHz] = EIRP [dBW] − FSPL [dB] − L_misc [dB] + G/T [dB/K] + 228.6

Derived quantities:

Eb/N₀_dB = C/N₀_dBHz − 10·log₁₀(R_bps)
C/N_dB = C/N₀_dBHz − 10·log₁₀(B_Hz)
Margin_dB = (C/N₀)_achieved − (C/N₀)_required

A positive margin means the link closes with headroom. Industry convention requires at least 2–3 dB of margin to account for implementation losses and parameter uncertainties.

THE FIVE TERMS EIRP — transmitter strength. FSPL — inverse-square-law loss. L_misc — all other losses (atmosphere, pointing, rain, interference — lessons 4–6). G/T — receiver quality. 228.6 — converts Boltzmann's constant to convenient units. Every link budget is these five terms, applied to the specific geometry and environment of the link.

The 15.5 dB clear-sky margin is the headroom before the required C/N₀ for this MODCOD is not met. Lessons m4-4, m4-5, and m4-6 add rain fade (7–20 dB at Ka-band), ionospheric effects, and interference — all of which spend this margin. Whether 15.5 dB is generous or barely adequate depends on the rain climate and the target availability, and adaptive systems (Module 5) convert whatever is left over in clear sky into higher-order MODCODs rather than wasting it.

CLEAR-SKY IS NOT THE DESIGN CASE The clear-sky link budget establishes the best-case performance. The design case is determined by the worst-case conditions that must still close with positive margin — typically the rain fade depth exceeded for some fraction of the year (e.g. 0.01% of time for 99.99% availability). Lessons m4-4 and m4-7 convert the clear-sky margin into a link availability statement.

Exercise 2 — Link Equation: EIRP, G/T, C/N₀

Compute C/N₀ and link margin for forward and return LEO Ka-band links.

import math

K_DB = -228.6   # dBW/K/Hz

def fspl(d_km, f_ghz):
    return 92.4 + 20*math.log10(d_km) + 20*math.log10(f_ghz)

def link_budget(name, p_tx_w, g_tx_dbi, d_km, f_ghz,
                losses_db, g_rx_dbi, t_sys_k, req_cn0):
    eirp   = 10*math.log10(p_tx_w) + g_tx_dbi
    loss   = fspl(d_km, f_ghz) + losses_db
    gt     = g_rx_dbi - 10*math.log10(t_sys_k)
    cn0    = eirp - loss + gt - K_DB
    margin = cn0 - req_cn0
    print(f"\n{name}")
    print(f"  EIRP   {eirp:+.1f} dBW  |  FSPL {fspl(d_km,f_ghz):.1f} dB  "
          f"|  G/T {gt:.1f} dB/K")
    print(f"  C/N₀   {cn0:.1f} dBHz  |  Required {req_cn0:.1f}  "
          f"|  Margin {margin:+.1f} dB  {'✓' if margin>=0 else '✗'}")
    return cn0, margin

# Forward link: satellite → user terminal
link_budget("FORWARD — Ka downlink (sat→terminal)",
    p_tx_w=20, g_tx_dbi=34,
    d_km=1000, f_ghz=20.0, losses_db=1.0,
    g_rx_dbi=37.5, t_sys_k=300, req_cn0=93.4)  # 16-APSK 3/4 @ 208.3 Msym/s

# Return link: user terminal → satellite
link_budget("RETURN — Ka uplink (terminal→sat)",
    p_tx_w=2, g_tx_dbi=35,
    d_km=1000, f_ghz=30.0, losses_db=1.5,
    g_rx_dbi=34, t_sys_k=500, req_cn0=87.2)   # QPSK 3/4 @ 208.3 Msym/s — return is tighter

Exercise 3 — Spine Stage 1: Link Budget Engine (Seed)

The extensible LinkBudget class that will be grown in m4-2 through m4-7. Each subsequent lesson adds one new contributor via add_loss() or an extended receiver noise chain.

import math

K_DB = -228.6

def fspl(d_km, f_ghz):
    return 92.4 + 20*math.log10(d_km) + 20*math.log10(f_ghz)

class LinkBudget:
    def __init__(self, name):
        self.name = name; self.rows = []
        self._eirp=self._fspl=self._grx=self._tsys=self._req=None

    def set_transmitter(self, p_tx_w, g_tx_dbi):
        self._eirp = 10*math.log10(p_tx_w) + g_tx_dbi
        self.rows += [("Tx power",       10*math.log10(p_tx_w), +1),
                      ("Tx antenna gain", g_tx_dbi,              +1)]

    def set_path(self, d_km, f_ghz):
        self._fspl = fspl(d_km, f_ghz)
        self.rows.append(("Free-space path loss", self._fspl, -1))

    def add_loss(self, label, loss_db):
        self.rows.append((label, loss_db, -1))

    def set_receiver(self, g_rx_dbi, t_sys_k):
        self._grx = g_rx_dbi; self._tsys = t_sys_k
        self.rows += [("Rx antenna gain",    g_rx_dbi,                   +1),
                      ("System noise temp",  10*math.log10(t_sys_k),     -1),
                      ("Boltzmann const",    abs(K_DB),                   +1)]

    def set_required(self, req):  self._req = req

    def compute(self):
        cn0 = (self._eirp - self._fspl + self._grx
               - 10*math.log10(self._tsys) - K_DB
               - sum(v for l,v,s in self.rows
                     if s==-1 and l not in
                     ("Free-space path loss","System noise temp")))
        return cn0, cn0 - self._req if self._req else None

    def print_budget(self):
        cn0, margin = self.compute()
        print(f"\n{'─'*54}\n  {self.name}\n{'─'*54}")
        for lbl, val, sign in self.rows:
            s = '+' if sign>0 else '−'
            print(f"  {s} {lbl:<35} {val:>7.2f} dB")
        print(f"{'─'*54}")
        print(f"    C/N₀            {cn0:>7.1f} dBHz")
        if self._req:
            print(f"    Required C/N₀   {self._req:>7.1f} dBHz")
            print(f"    Margin          {margin:>+7.1f} dB  "
                  f"{'✓ CLOSES' if margin>=0 else '✗ FAILS'}")
        print(f"{'─'*54}")

lb = LinkBudget("LEO Ka forward — clear sky")
lb.set_transmitter(20, 34)
lb.set_path(1000, 20.0)
lb.add_loss("Gaseous absorption", 0.3)
lb.add_loss("Pointing loss",      0.5)
lb.set_receiver(37.5, 300)
lb.set_required(93.4)  # 16-APSK 3/4 @ 208.3 Msym/s
lb.print_budget()
