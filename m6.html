User Terminals
& Direct-to-Device

Modules 4 and 5 consumed the terminal as three given numbers — a G/T, an EIRP, a pointing capability. This module builds the hardware behind them: parabolic reflectors and phased-array flat panels, the array factor and its grating lobes, analog versus digital beamforming, scan loss and the regulatory off-axis mask, pointing and handover dynamics, and the segment-and-certification wrapper that turns a €300 panel into a six-figure aviation unit. The spine is a Python terminal evaluator that runs every class — from the enterprise dish to the unpointed handset, 51 dB of G/T — back through the Module 4 link budget and Module 5 throughput engine. The finding: the dish's advantage is availability, not throughput.

Synthesis: The Terminal Trade Space
