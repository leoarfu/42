The Space Segment:
Spacecraft, Payload & Production

Module 2 designed the constellation geometry from the outside — how many satellites, in what orbits, at what cost. This module opens the satellite itself. It covers the bus subsystems that keep a satellite alive and pointed (power, structure, thermal, ADCS, and propulsion), the communications payload that generates the revenue (transparent vs regenerative architectures, digital channelisers, multibeam antennas, frequency reuse, and beam-hopping), and the manufacturing revolution that makes thousand-satellite constellations economically viable. Each lesson connects the subsystem physics to the constellation-level tradeoffs established in Module 2.

Bus Architecture: Power, Structure & Thermal

Solar array sizing and eclipse battery storage, structural load paths and fundamental frequency requirements, passive and active thermal control, radiator sizing, atomic oxygen erosion, and the integrated bus design problem. Mass-production philosophy vs traditional spacecraft reliability.

ADCS & Propulsion

Attitude determination (star trackers, IMUs, sun and Earth sensors) and control (reaction wheels, magnetic torquers, thrusters). On-orbit propulsion: the station-keeping and disposal Δv budget revisited from the bus perspective. Pointing budget and error allocation for a multibeam payload.

Part II — The Communications Payload
03

Payload Architectures: Transparent vs Regenerative

The bent-pipe (transparent) repeater and its limits. Regenerative (on-board processing) payloads: demodulate, switch, remodulate. Digital channelisers and flexible bandwidth allocation. Where processing lives in LEO broadband constellations and why. SDR payload fundamentals.

✓
04

Multibeam Antennas & Phased Arrays

From single-beam to multibeam: reflector feeds, direct radiating arrays, and electronically steered phased arrays. Array theory: element pattern, array factor, beam steering, grating lobes. Gain, half-power beamwidth, and sidelobe control. Active phased arrays for LEO user links.

✓
05

Frequency Reuse, Beam-Hopping & Flexible Payloads

Colour reuse patterns and interference geometry. Four-colour and aggressive reuse. Beam-hopping: time-division illumination across spots, hopping sequence design, and capacity redistribution. Software-defined and flexible payloads: reconfigurable power and bandwidth allocation on orbit.

Part III — Manufacturing at Scale

Manufacturing at Scale

The shift from artisanal spacecraft to production-line satellites: design for manufacturability, automated integration and test, statistical acceptance, supply chain for COTS components, and factory throughput models. Starlink's production rate as the benchmark. Cost-per-satellite drivers and the learning curve.
