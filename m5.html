Air Interface
& Access

Module 4 ended with a number in decibel-hertz; this module converts it into bits per second. It builds the conversion stage by stage: symbols and constellations shaped by the amplifier, the MODCOD ladder and adaptive coding that sit 1–2 dB from Shannon, the sharing of a carrier among hundreds of terminals, the framing that packages it all — then follows the two competing lineages, DVB-S2X and 3GPP NTN, to their convergence, and pushes the physics to its hardest case: an unmodified phone. The module spine is a Python throughput engine that consumes the Module 4 link budget and ends as the availability-throughput curve of a beam — for pointed dishes, NTN terminals, and smartphones alike.
