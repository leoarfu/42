Q1 — Global Connectivity & Multi-Orbit Context

1. Why Terrestrial Networks Break
Fiber and cellular infrastructure scale well in dense regions but collapse economically in oceans, deserts, air corridors, and polar routes. Aviation exposes this brutally: aircraft move between coverage zones every minutes, not hours. The limitation is not bandwidth — it’s geometry and economics.
2. GEO vs MEO vs LEO Reality
GEO provides stability and massive coverage but ~600ms RTT. MEO reduces latency but still relies on fewer satellites. LEO trades satellite count for latency (~20–40ms) and mobility complexity. As an architect, the shift is from “link design” to “dynamic constellation behavior”.
3. Aviation & Maritime Pain Points
Aircraft require seamless handover at 800 km/h while crossing beam footprints every few minutes. Maritime demands persistent coverage over sparse oceans. GEO beam hopping struggles here. LEO enables lower latency and smaller terminals but requires predictive routing.
4. Hybrid Architectures
The future is not LEO alone. It is hybrid: LEO for latency-sensitive traffic GEO for broadcast & bulk Terrestrial for dense metro Architectural skill lies in traffic steering and policy orchestration.
