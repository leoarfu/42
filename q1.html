Q1 — GEO vs LEO Systems Thinking (30 Hours)

0%

1. Global Connectivity Reality

Understand the physics of latency. GEO round-trip time averages ~600ms due to 35,786 km altitude. LEO at 1,200 km reduces RTT below 50ms. Map latency vs geography and compare to transatlantic fiber (~70ms).

Exercise: Calculate RTT for 550km LEO using speed of light and slant range.

~5 HOURS

2. Multi-Orbit Architecture

Compare GEO broadcast dominance vs LEO latency advantage. Understand regenerative payloads vs bent pipe. When does hybrid architecture outperform pure LEO?

Design exercise: Build connectivity architecture for global airline fleet.

~6 HOURS

3. Aviation & Maritime Constraints

Aircraft move at 900 km/h. Satellites at 7.6 km/s. Understand dual-motion tracking complexity. SWaP constraints for aero terminals. Polar routing challenges.

~6 HOURS

4. Gateway & Cloud Topology

Gateway placement must balance fiber adjacency and regulatory constraints. Design redundancy zones. Map traffic from aircraft → satellite → gateway → cloud PoP.

~7 HOURS

5. Why Mega-Constellations Exist

Capacity density math. Beam reuse vs altitude tradeoff. Launch cost economics. Why 3,000 satellites can outperform 3 GEO spacecraft for mobility.

~6 HOURS
