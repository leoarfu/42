Phase 1–2: Requirements and ConOps

The CDE's first job is translating what the customer wants to achieve into something Loft can actually execute. This means: understanding the mission objectives (what data, at what cadence, with what latency), defining the operational modes (nominal, contingency, safe), specifying the command sequences and scheduling constraints, and documenting all of this in a Concept of Operations (ConOps) document that both the customer and Loft's internal teams can work from.

ConOps for a Loft mission must cover: payload commanding sequences, data downlink windows and volumes, contact frequency requirements, anomaly response procedures, and the interface between the customer's ground tools and Cockpit's API. A CDE who writes a clear, complete ConOps makes every subsequent phase easier. A CDE who skips this creates ambiguity that surfaces as disputes during commissioning.

Phase 5: Commissioning — the CDE's most intense phase

Post-launch commissioning is when the CDE transitions from planner to operator. After the satellite achieves orbit, Loft's team verifies the bus and Hub are nominal, then the CDE leads the payload commissioning: powering on the payload, verifying interfaces, running first light tests, and validating that the mission application works as designed against the ConOps. Madeline Fleury (your next interviewer) was payload commissioning lead for YAM-9. This phase typically runs 2–6 weeks and is where most mission-specific surprises appear.

Phase 6: On-orbit operations — the steady state

Once commissioned, the CDE owns the mission day-to-day. This means: monitoring telemetry for anomalies, responding to customer requests for schedule changes or additional operations, managing the Cockpit interface for the customer's operators, and being the primary contact when anything doesn't work as expected. At Loft's scale — 30+ missions on a growing fleet — automation is essential. A CDE who manually manages every task cannot scale.

3. Anomaly Triage: The CDE Mental Model

When something stops working on orbit, the CDE is the first responder. The triage methodology is the same layered approach you used at iDirect — but the layers are different. You must be able to describe your reasoning process fluently, because Bella will almost certainly ask you a scenario question.

The five-layer fault isolation model for satellite ops

  1. Ground segment: Is the problem on the ground? Check ground station contact logs — did the pass complete? Check Cockpit connectivity — is the ground software reachable? This is always step one. Most "satellite problems" are ground problems.
  2. Link layer: Was the uplink/downlink established? Check received signal level, Eb/N0, frame error rate. A link margin issue manifests as intermittent commanding or telemetry gaps. Your iDirect background covers this layer cold.
  3. Bus telemetry: Is the satellite bus nominal? EPS (power) state — are the batteries charged, are the solar panels oriented? ADCS — is attitude nominal? Thermal — is any subsystem above limits? Bus anomalies are rarely the CDE's fault but they affect the mission.
  4. Hub / payload interface: Is the Hub reporting the payload as healthy? Loft's Hub provides a standardised interface; Hub telemetry tells you whether the payload side of the interface is up.
  5. Payload itself: If the Hub interface is up but the payload isn't responding correctly, the fault is in the payload. This is where the CDE works with the customer to diagnose — what is the payload's last known state? Has it been commanded recently? Is there an application-layer issue?
THE IIDIRECT PARALLEL — USE THIS LANGUAGE At iDirect you isolated across RF → DVB-S2 modem → IP layer → customer application. The CDE stack is: ground segment → RF link → bus → Hub → payload. Same methodology, same discipline, different hardware labels. When Bella asks how you approach anomaly triage, walk her through your iDirect methodology and then map it to the satellite ops context. Show her you already think this way.

What to communicate to the customer during an anomaly

While diagnosing, you owe the customer timely, factual updates. Not "we're looking into it." Specifically: what you know (observable symptoms), what you've ruled out (layers already checked), what you're investigating next, and a timeline for the next update. Customers who receive structured updates during an outage are significantly less anxious than those who receive reassuring but vague status messages.

4. Owning a Customer: The Relationship Layer

The CDE is the customer's primary technical contact. This means managing expectations before problems occur, not only during them. A CDE who waits for the customer to report problems is reactive. A CDE who monitors proactively and contacts the customer with early warnings before they notice is a CDE who builds trust.

Managing expectation gaps

The most common source of customer dissatisfaction is not system failures — it is expectation gaps. The customer expected X; the system delivered Y; nobody told them why Y was acceptable or how it compared to X. The CDE's role is to close these gaps before they become complaints. This requires: understanding what the customer actually cares about (not just what the ConOps says), monitoring the metrics that matter to them, and communicating proactively when those metrics deviate.

Escalation vs ownership

Know which problems to own and which to escalate. A CDE should own: customer communication, first-level anomaly triage, ConOps interpretation, scheduling decisions. A CDE should escalate: bus-level anomalies requiring flight ops intervention, changes to the satellite's operating mode that affect other missions, hardware issues requiring engineering analysis. The failure mode to avoid is escalating too early and making Bella or the ops team solve problems you could solve yourself.

QUESTION BELLA WILL ASK — AND THE ANSWER "Tell me about a time a customer was unhappy and how you handled it." This is not a trap — it is a test of maturity. The answer should: acknowledge the customer's reality (not dismiss it), describe what you communicated and when, explain what you found and fixed, and describe what you put in place to prevent recurrence. Do not tell a story where everything was fine — tell one where something was genuinely hard and you handled it well.

Exercise 1 — Telemetry Anomaly Detector

Parse a satellite telemetry stream, detect values outside normal operating bounds, classify anomaly severity, and generate a structured incident report. This is the kind of script a CDE builds to avoid staring at dashboards.

import json
from datetime import datetime, timedelta
from dataclasses import dataclass, field
from typing import List, Dict, Optional
import random

# --- Telemetry Schema ---
# Each telemetry point: {timestamp, channel_id, value, unit}
# Channels defined with nominal range and severity thresholds

@dataclass
class TelemetryChannel:
    channel_id: str
    description: str
    unit: str
    nominal_min: float
    nominal_max: float
    warn_min: float        # below this → WARNING
    warn_max: float        # above this → WARNING
    crit_min: float        # below this → CRITICAL
    crit_max: float        # above this → CRITICAL

@dataclass
class Anomaly:
    timestamp: str
    channel_id: str
    description: str
    value: float
    unit: str
    severity: str          # 'WARNING' or 'CRITICAL'
    threshold_violated: str

CHANNELS = {
    'EPS_BATT_SOC':   TelemetryChannel('EPS_BATT_SOC',   'Battery State of Charge', '%',   30, 100, 20, 100, 10, 100),
    'THERM_PAYLOAD':  TelemetryChannel('THERM_PAYLOAD',   'Payload Temperature',     'degC',-10,  55,  -15, 60, -20, 70),
    'ADCS_ANGLE_ERR': TelemetryChannel('ADCS_ANGLE_ERR',  'Attitude Error',          'deg',   0,   1,   0,  2,   0,  5),
    'COMMS_RSSI':     TelemetryChannel('COMMS_RSSI',      'Uplink RSSI',             'dBm', -90, -50,-100,-50,-110,-50),
    'HUB_PAYLOAD_HB': TelemetryChannel('HUB_PAYLOAD_HB',  'Hub Payload Heartbeat',   'bool',  1,   1,   1,  1,   1,  1),
}

def generate_test_telemetry(n_points: int = 200) -> List[Dict]:
    """Generate a synthetic telemetry stream with injected anomalies."""
    telem = []
    base_time = datetime(2026, 4, 2, 10, 0, 0)
    # Inject anomaly windows
    anomaly_windows = {
        'EPS_BATT_SOC':   (80, 120, 15),    # low battery around t=80-120
        'THERM_PAYLOAD':  (150, 170, 62),   # overtemp around t=150-170
        'ADCS_ANGLE_ERR': (190, 200, 3.2),  # attitude exceedance near end
    }
    for i in range(n_points):
        ts = (base_time + timedelta(seconds=i*30)).isoformat()
        for ch_id, ch in CHANNELS.items():
            if ch_id == 'HUB_PAYLOAD_HB':
                val = 1.0 if i < 185 else 0.0   # heartbeat loss near end
            elif ch_id in anomaly_windows:
                start, end, bad_val = anomaly_windows[ch_id]
                if start <= i < end:
                    val = bad_val + random.gauss(0, 0.5)
                else:
                    val = random.uniform(ch.nominal_min + (ch.nominal_max-ch.nominal_min)*0.3,
                                        ch.nominal_max - (ch.nominal_max-ch.nominal_min)*0.1)
            else:
                val = random.uniform(ch.nominal_min + 5, ch.nominal_max - 5)
            telem.append({'timestamp': ts, 'channel_id': ch_id,
                         'value': round(val, 3), 'unit': ch.unit})
    return telem

def classify_anomaly(ch: TelemetryChannel, value: float) -> Optional[tuple]:
    """Return (severity, threshold_description) or None if nominal."""
    if value <= ch.crit_min or value >= ch.crit_max:
        direction = 'below' if value <= ch.crit_min else 'above'
        threshold = ch.crit_min if value <= ch.crit_min else ch.crit_max
        return ('CRITICAL', f'{direction} critical threshold {threshold}{ch.unit}')
    if value <= ch.warn_min or value >= ch.warn_max:
        direction = 'below' if value <= ch.warn_min else 'above'
        threshold = ch.warn_min if value <= ch.warn_min else ch.warn_max
        return ('WARNING', f'{direction} warning threshold {threshold}{ch.unit}')
    return None

def process_telemetry(telem_stream: List[Dict]) -> List[Anomaly]:
    """Process telemetry stream and return list of anomalies (deduplicated by channel)."""
    anomalies = []
    active = {}   # channel_id → last anomaly severity (suppress repeated reports)
    for point in telem_stream:
        ch_id = point['channel_id']
        if ch_id not in CHANNELS:
            continue
        ch = CHANNELS[ch_id]
        result = classify_anomaly(ch, point['value'])
        if result:
            severity, description = result
            # Only report if severity changed or new
            if active.get(ch_id) != severity:
                anomalies.append(Anomaly(
                    timestamp=point['timestamp'],
                    channel_id=ch_id,
                    description=ch.description,
                    value=point['value'],
                    unit=point['unit'],
                    severity=severity,
                    threshold_violated=description
                ))
                active[ch_id] = severity
        else:
            if ch_id in active:
                print(f"  [RECOVERY] {ch_id} returned to nominal at {point['timestamp']}")
                del active[ch_id]
    return anomalies

def generate_incident_report(mission_id: str, anomalies: List[Anomaly]) -> str:
    """Format a structured incident report for customer communication."""
    criticals = [a for a in anomalies if a.severity == 'CRITICAL']
    warnings   = [a for a in anomalies if a.severity == 'WARNING']
    lines = [
        f"MISSION ANOMALY REPORT — {mission_id}",
        f"Generated: {datetime.utcnow().isoformat()}Z",
        f"{'='*60}",
        f"SUMMARY: {len(criticals)} CRITICAL  |  {len(warnings)} WARNING",
        "",
    ]
    for sev, group in [('CRITICAL', criticals), ('WARNING', warnings)]:
        if group:
            lines.append(f"--- {sev} EVENTS ---")
            for a in group:
                lines.append(f"  [{a.timestamp}] {a.channel_id} ({a.description})")
                lines.append(f"    Value: {a.value} {a.unit}")
                lines.append(f"    Condition: {a.threshold_violated}")
                lines.append("")
    lines += [
        "RECOMMENDED ACTIONS:",
        "  1. Confirm ground pass coverage during anomaly window",
        "  2. Review full telemetry dump for correlated channels",
        "  3. Assess payload operational impact and notify customer",
        "  4. Initiate anomaly resolution procedure per ConOps §5.2",
    ]
    return "\n".join(lines)

# --- Run ---
print("Generating synthetic telemetry stream...")
telem = generate_test_telemetry(200)
print(f"Processing {len(telem)} telemetry points across {len(CHANNELS)} channels...")
print()

anomalies = process_telemetry(telem)
report = generate_incident_report("MISSION-YAM9-PAYLOAD-A", anomalies)
print(report)

Exercise 2 — Ground Pass Health Checker

Given a schedule of expected ground station passes, check which passes completed successfully and flag gaps. Simulates the first step of anomaly triage: "did the pass even happen?"

from datetime import datetime, timedelta
from dataclasses import dataclass
from typing import List, Tuple
import random

@dataclass
class PassRecord:
    pass_id: str
    ground_station: str
    expected_start: datetime
    expected_end: datetime
    actual_start: datetime | None
    actual_end: datetime | None
    max_elevation_deg: float
    frames_received: int
    frames_expected: int

    @property
    def completed(self) -> bool:
        return self.actual_start is not None and self.actual_end is not None

    @property
    def frame_success_rate(self) -> float:
        if self.frames_expected == 0: return 0.0
        return self.frames_received / self.frames_expected

    @property
    def duration_actual_s(self) -> float:
        if not self.completed: return 0
        return (self.actual_end - self.actual_start).total_seconds()

    @property
    def duration_expected_s(self) -> float:
        return (self.expected_end - self.expected_start).total_seconds()

    def status(self) -> str:
        if not self.completed:
            return "MISSED"
        if self.frame_success_rate < 0.5:
            return "DEGRADED"
        if self.duration_actual_s < self.duration_expected_s * 0.8:
            return "PARTIAL"
        return "NOMINAL"

def generate_pass_schedule(n_passes: int = 24) -> List[PassRecord]:
    """Simulate 24 hours of ground station passes."""
    stations = ['Toulouse-GS', 'AWS-Punta-Arenas', 'AWS-Svalbard', 'Inuvik-GS']
    passes = []
    t = datetime(2026, 4, 2, 0, 0, 0)
    for i in range(n_passes):
        gs = stations[i % len(stations)]
        duration = timedelta(minutes=random.uniform(6, 14))
        expected_start = t
        expected_end = t + duration
        # Inject failures
        if i in [3, 11, 19]:  # missed passes
            actual_start, actual_end = None, None
            frames_rx = 0
        elif i in [7, 15]:    # degraded passes
            actual_start = expected_start + timedelta(seconds=random.uniform(0, 30))
            actual_end   = actual_start + duration * 0.4
            frames_rx = int(random.uniform(20, 45))
        else:                  # nominal
            actual_start = expected_start + timedelta(seconds=random.uniform(0, 10))
            actual_end   = actual_start + duration * random.uniform(0.92, 1.0)
            frames_rx = int(random.uniform(90, 100))
        passes.append(PassRecord(
            pass_id=f"PASS-{i+1:03d}",
            ground_station=gs,
            expected_start=expected_start,
            expected_end=expected_end,
            actual_start=actual_start,
            actual_end=actual_end,
            max_elevation_deg=random.uniform(15, 85),
            frames_received=frames_rx,
            frames_expected=100,
        ))
        t += timedelta(hours=1, minutes=random.uniform(-10, 10))
    return passes

def analyse_passes(passes: List[PassRecord]) -> dict:
    """Compute pass health statistics."""
    statuses = [p.status() for p in passes]
    return {
        'total':    len(passes),
        'nominal':  statuses.count('NOMINAL'),
        'partial':  statuses.count('PARTIAL'),
        'degraded': statuses.count('DEGRADED'),
        'missed':   statuses.count('MISSED'),
        'success_rate': statuses.count('NOMINAL') / len(passes) if passes else 0,
    }

# --- Run ---
passes = generate_pass_schedule(24)
stats = analyse_passes(passes)

print("24-HOUR PASS HEALTH REPORT")
print(f"{'='*56}")
print(f"Total passes: {stats['total']}  |  "
      f"Nominal: {stats['nominal']}  |  "
      f"Partial: {stats['partial']}  |  "
      f"Degraded: {stats['degraded']}  |  "
      f"Missed: {stats['missed']}")
print(f"Overall pass success rate: {stats['success_rate']*100:.1f}%")
print()

non_nominal = [p for p in passes if p.status() != 'NOMINAL']
if non_nominal:
    print("NON-NOMINAL PASSES:")
    for p in non_nominal:
        print(f"  [{p.status():8s}] {p.pass_id} | {p.ground_station:20s} | "
              f"El={p.max_elevation_deg:.0f}° | "
              f"Frames={p.frames_received}/{p.frames_expected} | "
              f"Expected {p.expected_start.strftime('%H:%M')}–{p.expected_end.strftime('%H:%M')}")
else:
    print("All passes nominal.")
