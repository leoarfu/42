Orbital Mechanics & Constellations 101

MARK COMPLETE

Quarter Objectives

Master the "moving target" reality of LEO that never existed in GEO. Build intuition for why handovers happen every few minutes, why Doppler is significant, and how constellations achieve global coverage without stationary satellites.

By the end: Run your first Python orbit simulations and visualize ground tracks & visibility windows.

Core Topics & Sub-Sections

1. Foundational Orbital Physics

  • Newton’s law of gravitation & centripetal force balance
  • Kepler’s three laws (focus on 1st & 3rd for period vs altitude)
  • Orbital elements: semi-major axis, eccentricity, inclination, RAAN, argument of perigee, true anomaly
  • Vis-viva equation & specific mechanical energy

2. LEO vs GEO/MEO Regimes

  • Altitude ranges & typical velocities (~7.6 km/s in LEO)
  • Orbital period formula (T = 2π √(a³/μ))
  • Ground track patterns & nodal precession due to J2 perturbation
  • Why LEO needs many satellites for continuous coverage

3. Visibility, Passes & Handovers

  • Satellite visibility cone & elevation angle
  • Pass duration & maximum elevation calculations
  • Revisit time & coverage gap analysis
  • Handover frequency in LEO (~every 5–10 min)

4. Constellation Fundamentals

  • Walker Delta & Walker Star patterns
  • Number of planes, satellites per plane, inclination/phasing
  • Streets-of-coverage method
  • Basic coverage probability & quality metrics

5. Tools & Data Sources

  • SGP4 propagator & Two-Line Element (TLE) format
  • Celestrak / Space-Track for real satellite ephemeris
