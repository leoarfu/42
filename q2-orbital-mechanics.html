Core Engineering Blocks

1. Orbital Energy & Velocity — Why LEO Moves So Fast

Two-body dynamics foundation

For circular orbit:

v = √(μ / r)

μ = GM ≈ 3.986 × 10¹⁴ m³/s² r = Rₑ + h

Specific orbital energy

ε = - μ / (2a)

Lower orbit → more negative energy → deeper gravity well → higher velocity.

Engineering implications

  • 550 km LEO ≈ 7.6 km/s
  • GEO ≈ 3.07 km/s
  • Velocity drives Doppler (~±40 kHz at Ku/Ka)
  • Velocity drives rapid link geometry evolution
Essential: LEO latency advantage comes from low altitude. Low altitude requires high velocity. High velocity drives every systems problem downstream.

2. Kepler's Third Law — Period & Revisit Mechanics

T = 2π √(a³ / μ)

Numbers you must know cold

  • 400–600 km LEO → ~90–97 min
  • MEO (GPS) → ~12 hours
  • GEO → 23h 56m (sidereal day)

Constellation insight

LEO satellite completes ~15 orbits/day. Earth rotates beneath it. Ground track shifts west each orbit.

Essential: Period determines revisit time, constellation phasing, and repeat-ground-track design.

3. J₂ Effect — Nodal Precession & Plane Drift

dΩ/dt = - (3/2) n J₂ (Rₑ / p)² cos i

Earth is not a perfect sphere. Oblateness causes RAAN drift.

Why this matters for LEO networks

  • Constellation planes drift relative to each other
  • Sun-synchronous orbits exploit J₂ intentionally
  • Phasing maintenance requires propulsion
Essential: J₂ is not “theory.” It directly affects long-term constellation geometry stability.

4. Atmospheric Drag & Lifetime Engineering

a_drag ∝ ρ v² (A/m)

LEO reality

  • 400 km → strong drag
  • 550 km → moderate drag
  • Solar cycle increases density

System implications

  • Constellations must continuously raise orbit
  • Fuel budget defines satellite lifetime
  • Drag causes semi-major axis decay → period changes
Essential: Drag makes LEO dynamic. Unlike GEO, nothing “just stays there.”
